## Classification

Finding.

After PR `#739`, direct workflows in `case-001` made progress while many
debouncer rows were active, but unrelated DB-backed queued workflows in
`case-002` still did not satisfy the liveness contract under a tiny executor
thread pool. The failure reproduced in a focused case-only cloud replay, so it
is not an artifact of sequential case history.

The product-facing symptom is that an active hot-key debouncer can coexist with
an unrelated queue whose trivial workflows only become observable through
handles after roughly the hot debounce window, violating the rung promise that
active debounce windows must not starve unrelated queued workflows.
