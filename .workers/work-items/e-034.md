# E-034 recv Delivery Stalls 60s on a Missed Notification (S5, availability)

## Product Promise (availability facet of `notifications-deliver-exactly-once`)

A recipient blocked in `DBOS.recv` is delivered a durably-committed message
promptly. Delivery is driven by a LISTEN/NOTIFY listener; a 60s DB-poll fallback
(`_notification_fallback_polling_interval`, `_sys_db.py:3071`) exists as a
documented "safety net against dropped notifications."

## Mechanism (source-grounded)

`send` commits a `dbos.notifications` row; an AFTER-INSERT trigger
`dbos_notifications_trigger` fires `pg_notify('dbos_notifications_channel', …)`
(`_migration.py:314`); the listener thread (LISTEN, `_sys_db_postgres.py:169`)
sets the recv waiter's in-memory event. If that async notification is lost — the
listener's connection resetting across a DB reconnect — the event is never set,
and recv's wait loop uses `_event_recheck_interval()` = **60s** (with a listener
running, `:3082`) before it re-polls the DB (`event.wait(timeout=min(remaining,
recheck_interval))`, `:3112`). So a message durably available at t≈0 is not
delivered until ~min(recv_timeout, 60s). `recv_consume`'s final transactional
poll means no loss — but up to a 60s availability stall.

## Verdict

**RED (availability characterization, weight 2) — deterministic.** The workload
models the missed notification faithfully and WITHOUT netem timing luck: DISABLE
the trigger, `DBOS.send` (row commits, no `pg_notify`), re-enable — the exact
recv-side state a dropped NOTIFY produces. Local (pg :5459):
- case-001 control (trigger enabled): recv delivered in **2.0s**. GREEN.
- case-002 missed-notify (trigger disabled during send): recv delivered in
  **60.025s** — exactly the fallback interval. Delivered (no loss), 30× latency.

Differential: an identical durably-committed message, delivered 2s vs 60s purely
because the async signal was missed. This is the S5 corridor (parked at score 5,
UNPARKED for Wave 2). It is a documented-fallback latency degradation, not an
exactly-once or data-loss defect — hence classified **characterization**, not a
finding_candidate. Low filing value on its own (the 60s fallback is deliberate);
recorded as the availability facet of the notification path under reconnect.
Filing decision (if any) stays with Viswa.
